As a plant ecophysiologist, my work involves exploring the complex physiological processes that occur within plants, and investigating the mechanisms by which they interact with their surroundings. Through my research, I hope to gain a deeper understanding of how plants are able to adapt and thrive in various environmental conditions, and to contribute to the development of more sustainable agricultural practices. Overall, my work as a plant ecophysiologist represents a crucial area of research, as it has the potential to unlock new insights into how we can better understand and protect our natural ecosystems. To tackle these questions I use geneticaly encoded fluorescent reporters to track cellular calcium & ROS dynamics.
